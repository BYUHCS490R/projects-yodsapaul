Structural Tags

HEADER, NAV, MAIN, FOOTER

What goes in the header?

The header is at the top of the page. It shows the main identity of the website, like the website name and logo. It can also include an H1 title for the website. This helps Google understand what the website is.

What goes in the Nav? Can the Nav go in the header? Does it have to go in the header? Does the Nav contain links to another website?

- The Nav is the menu with the main links. Like Home, About, Contact.

- Yes, the Nav can be inside the header, but it does not have to.

- The Nav links should mostly go to pages in the same website, not to a different website.

What goes in Main?

The Main Tag is the most important part of the page. It has the main content people are reading. This part changes when you go to different pages. It tells search engines what this page is mainly about.

What goes in the Footer?

The Footer is at the bottom of the page. It usually has extra stuffs like copyrights or additional links.

HTML Validation

HTML Validation is an action that checking any HTML mistakes. If your tags are wrong (like a wrong closing tag). The browser and Google may get confused and guess your meaning (W3C HTML Validator).
